Glossary of Terms

Key terms used in ISO-NE transmission ratemaking and this dashboard.

Rate Concepts

ATRR — Annual Transmission Revenue Requirement
The total annual amount a transmission owner is authorized to collect from load-serving entities via the transmission tariff. Equals ratebase × carrying charge rate, covering return on equity, debt service, depreciation, and taxes.
RNS — Regional Network Service
Transmission service covering use of the bulk regional (PTF) system. RNS costs are allocated to all loads in the ISO-NE region based on each zone's share of regional peak demand.
LNS — Local Network Service
Transmission service covering lower-voltage (sub-PTF) facilities. Unlike RNS, LNS costs are borne by the load in the specific zone served — not allocated regionwide.
Schedule 12C
An ISO-NE OATT tariff schedule for certain transmission facilities whose costs are recovered from specific beneficiaries rather than allocated across the region. United Illuminating (UI) and Eversource entities file Schedule 12C ATRRs.
PTF — Pool Transmission Facilities
High-voltage transmission facilities (generally 69 kV and above) that are centrally dispatched by ISO-NE. PTF costs are shared regionwide under the ISO-NE Open Access Transmission Tariff (OATT). PTF plant in service is the ratebase numerator for RNS carrying charge calculations.
RNS Rate ($/kW-yr)
The annual per-kilowatt charge applied to each load zone's coincident peak demand to recover the regional RNS ATRR. Calculated as: Regional RNS ATRR ÷ Total Regional Coincident Peak Load. Effective January 1 each year (post-2022).

Financial & Regulatory Terms

Carrying Charge
The annualized rate (% of PTF plant) representing total allowed annual cost per dollar of transmission investment: equity return + debt cost + depreciation + taxes. A higher carrying charge means more annual cost per dollar of capital. Varies by TO based on capital structure, tax rate, and ROE.
ROE — Return on Equity
The allowed annual rate of return on the equity component of a TO's capital structure. Set by FERC for most ISO-NE TOs via the formula rate; set by Vermont state regulators for VELCO.
Base ROE
The foundational allowed ROE before adders. For ISO-NE formula rate TOs: 10.57% through February 2026, reduced to 9.57% by FERC order (March 2026, EL11-66 et al.).
RTO Adder
A 0.50% increment added to the Base ROE for all ISO-NE TOs as compensation for participating in a Regional Transmission Organization (per FERC Policy Statement, 2008). Applied to all formula-rate TOs except VELCO.
All-in Base ROE
Base ROE plus the RTO adder. Prior to March 2026 order: 10.57% + 0.50% = 11.07%. Post-order: 9.57% + 0.50% = 10.07%. Embedded in formula rates pending tariff compliance filings.
Investment Base (Ratebase)
The net transmission investment on which the allowed return is calculated. Approximately equals net PTF plant plus working capital, averaged over 5 quarters (WS 1, Line 13 of the formula rate). Used to determine the ATRR across all rate schedules (RNS + LNS + Sched 12C).
Formula Rate
A FERC-approved tariff mechanism that sets annual transmission rates using a standardized formula applied to each TO's own financial data. Updated annually based on prior-year actuals and near-term forecasts. ISO-NE's Settled Formula Rate took effect in 2022 (Docket ER20-2054).

Entities & Dockets

PTO AC — Participating Transmission Owner Administrative Committee
The ISO-NE committee of transmission owners responsible for preparing the annual RNS rate update presentation and filing the Annual Informational Update with FERC. Source of the primary data used in this dashboard.
NEPOOL — New England Power Pool
The organization of market participants operating within the ISO-NE region. The RC/TC (Reliability Committee/Transmission Committee) is a NEPOOL stakeholder forum where the PTO AC presents annual rate updates.
FERC — Federal Energy Regulatory Commission
The federal agency that regulates interstate transmission rates under the Federal Power Act. FERC reviews and approves formula rate changes and adjudicates ROE complaints (such as EL11-66).
EL11-66 (et al.)
FERC dockets for the ISO-New England ROE complaint proceedings (EL11-66, EL14-12, EL15-45). The March 2026 FERC order reduced the base ROE from 10.57% to 9.57%, increased the ROE cap from 11.74% to 12.09%, and established a refund period (Oct. 2011 – Dec. 2012).
ER20-2054
The FERC docket for the ISO-NE Settled Formula Rate proceeding. The PTO AC files annual informational updates in this docket each July 31. This dashboard's LNS, Schedule 12C, ratebase, and capital structure data are sourced from the July 31, 2025 filing.
ATT VT-1
The rate attachment in VELCO's (Vermont Transco LLC) tariff that establishes its Vermont state-regulated ROE of 11.74%. VELCO's ROE is set by Vermont state regulators, not by FERC under the ISO-NE formula rate, and is not subject to the EL11-66 ROE reduction.
Settled Formula Rate
The negotiated formula rate mechanism effective January 1, 2022 (Docket ER20-2054), replacing the prior Annual Informational Filing process. Under the Settled Formula Rate, RNS rates take effect January 1 (changed from June 1), and all 6 major TOs file under a standardized Appendix B format.

Regional Network Service (RNS) Cost per kWh by Utility Territory

The 2026 RNS rate is $183.71/kW-yr — a wholesale charge applied to load-serving entities based on their ISO-NE coincident peak contribution. This section estimates how that cost translates to average cents per kWh for customers in each New England utility territory.

Methodology:
  1. Load Ratio Share (LRS) estimated from 2025 CELT 90/10 summer peak forecast by ISO-NE load zone (CELT sheet "6.2 Forecasts for Trans Pln," 2025 column). Regional total: 27,828 MW.
  2. For zones with multiple retail utilities (CT: CL&P + UI; ME: CMP + Versant), zone LRS split proportionally by each utility's 2024 residential kWh sales (EIA Form 861, 2024).
  3. Annual RNS cost per utility = utility LRS × $3,241.6M (6-TO regional RNS ATRR, 2024-based; full regional total = $3,331.2M).
  4. Total retail kWh estimated from EIA-861 residential sales using state residential fraction benchmarks (CT 35%, ME 43%, NH 37%, RI 38%). RNS ¢/kWh = annual RNS cost ÷ estimated total retail kWh.
  5. Annual household cost assumes 600 kWh/month (7,200 kWh/yr, approximate New England residential average). Actual residential bills depend on each state's retail tariff rate design.

Asset Condition Reviewer (ACR) — Process in Development

The oversight gap: Asset condition projects are TO-initiated and their costs are socialized to all New England ratepayers via the regional RNS rate — without a pre-construction independent reliability determination. As New England's transmission infrastructure ages and asset condition investment has accelerated, this lack of independent pre-construction review has become a significant policy concern for state consumer advocates and regulators across the region. The ACR is the negotiated policy response.

What the ACR Will (and Won't) Do

  • ISO-NE takes an advisory, non-binding review role over TO asset condition investment proposals
  • ISO-NE will not make go/no-go decisions; TOs retain final investment authority
  • Provides the first independent technical assessment of project need and scope
  • Interim threshold: projects with >$25M in regionalized costs subject to interim review (2025–2026)
  • Target permanent implementation: January 2027
  • ISO-NE 2026 budget allocation: ~$1M
  • ACR will produce a public record of pre-construction review even if non-binding

Policy and Regulatory Context

  • The scope of the ACR role — advisory vs. binding — was itself contested in stakeholder negotiations; the advisory-only outcome reflects negotiated limits with the TOs
  • New England consumer advocates (Maine Office of Public Advocate, other NECUCA members) filed a FERC challenge in Jan. 2024 arguing TOs refused to provide investment policy data (Docket ER20-2054)
  • ACR Key Project page: iso-ne.com/…/asset-condition-reviewer
  • Quarterly Asset Condition List: iso-ne.com — RSP & ACL

Current data limitation: The ACL tracks estimated project costs and status but does not include asset condition severity scores, equipment age, or alternatives analysis. The ACR process is expected to generate more structured project justification data over time. Until then, quantitative analysis of whether asset condition spending is appropriately sized requires TO-specific data requests or FERC rate case discovery.
